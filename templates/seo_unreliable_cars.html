What This Means for March 2026 MOTs

Every March sees a surge in first-time MOTs as vehicles registered on the previous March plates hit their third birthday. For cars registered in March 2023, that milestone arrives in March 2026. This creates one of the busiest periods of the year for testing stations — and for drivers who have never had to deal with MOT preparation before.

If you own one of the models listed above, there is no need to panic. A high failure rate does not mean your car will definitely fail. It means that, statistically, your model has a higher proportion of vehicles that present with defects at their first MOT compared to other models. The most common reasons for first-MOT failures are straightforward: lighting faults (blown bulbs, misaligned headlights), tyre wear, and brake wear from high early mileage.

Here is what we recommend for owners of these vehicles:

  • Book early: Do not leave your MOT until the last day of the month. Book 2-4 weeks ahead of your due date so you have time for repairs if needed. Remember, you can have your MOT done up to one month (minus a day) before expiry without losing any time on your certificate.
  • Walk-around check: Spend 10 minutes checking all your lights, tyre tread depths, wiper blades, and windscreen for chips. These are the most common first-MOT failure points and the cheapest to fix yourself.
  • Check your advisories: If this is not your car's first MOT, review any advisories from the previous test. Items flagged as advisory last time may have deteriorated enough to become a failure this time around.
  • Consider a pre-MOT inspection: A pre-MOT check at a local garage typically costs between £20 and £30. It is the cheapest insurance against an unexpected failure and gives you time to shop around for competitive repair prices.
  • Focus on your model's weak spots: Use the component breakdowns above to see where your specific model is most likely to have issues, and pay extra attention to those areas during your pre-MOT checks.

Why do some new cars fail their first MOT?

Even relatively new cars can fail their first MOT at 3 years old. The most common reasons include lighting and electrical faults (blown bulbs, misaligned headlights), tyre wear below the legal 1.6mm minimum, and brake wear — particularly on vehicles that have covered high mileage in their first three years. Some models have design-related weaknesses that show up early, such as premature suspension wear or electrical issues.

How can I prepare my car for its first MOT?

To prepare your car for its first MOT, check all lights and bulbs are working, ensure tyre tread depth is above 1.6mm across the central three-quarters of the tread, inspect wiper blades for wear, top up windscreen washer fluid, and check for any warning lights on the dashboard. A pre-MOT inspection at a local garage (typically £20-£30) can identify issues before the official test. Book your MOT 2-4 weeks before the due date to allow time for any necessary repairs.
