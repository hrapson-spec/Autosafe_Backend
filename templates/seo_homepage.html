Frequently Asked Questions

Will my car pass its MOT?

Whether your car will pass depends on its age, mileage, make, model, and previous test history. The UK average failure rate is approximately {{ "%.0f"|format(national_avg_fail_rate * 100) }}%, meaning nearly 1 in 3 vehicles fail. How does AutoSafe predict MOT failures?

AutoSafe uses a CatBoost machine learning model trained on over {{ "{:,}".format(total_tests_analysed) }} official DVSA MOT test records. The model analyses 104 features — including vehicle age, mileage, make and model patterns, previous test results, advisory trends, and regional corrosion factors — to predict failure probability with calibrated accuracy.

Is AutoSafe really free?

Yes, completely. Enter your registration number and postcode for an instant, personalised MOT failure risk prediction covering seven key failure areas. No signup, no payment, no catch.

What is the most common reason for MOT failure?

Lighting and signalling issues are the single biggest cause of MOT failures, including blown bulbs, misaligned headlights, and faulty indicators. Suspension problems and brake defects are the second and third most common reasons. See our top 10 MOT failures guide for details.

How much does an MOT cost?

The maximum MOT fee is £54.85 for cars. Many garages charge less. However, the real cost includes potential repair bills if you fail — averaging £150–£500 depending on defects found. See our MOT cost guide for a full breakdown.
