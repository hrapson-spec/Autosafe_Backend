March 2026 MOT Rush — Which Cars Are Most at Risk?

March is the UK’s busiest month for MOT tests. Here’s what the data says about which 2023-plate cars are most likely to fail their first MOT.

Why March Is the UK’s Busiest MOT Month

Every March and September, the UK number plate changes. March is historically the biggest month for new car registrations — in 2023 alone, hundreds of thousands of new cars hit the road with 23-plates.

Under UK law, new vehicles must have their first MOT exactly 3 years after registration. That means the wave of March 2023 registrations will all need their first MOT in March 2026, creating the year’s biggest rush at testing stations across the country.

This matters for two reasons: (1) testing stations fill up fast, so book early to avoid delays, and (2) even relatively new cars can and do fail their first MOT. The data below shows which models are most at risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is March 2026 the busiest MOT month?

March is historically the UK’s biggest month for new car registrations due to the spring plate change. Cars registered in March 2023 (23-plate) reach their mandatory 3-year first MOT in March 2026, creating a surge of first-time MOT tests at stations across the country.

When is my car’s first MOT due?

New cars need their first MOT exactly 3 years after registration. If your car was registered in March 2023, its first MOT is due in March 2026. You can take the test up to one month early without losing any time on the certificate — so booking in February is a smart way to beat the rush.

Can a brand new car fail its first MOT?

Yes. While first-MOT failure rates are lower than for older vehicles, they are not zero. Common issues include blown bulbs, worn tyres, windscreen chips, and wiper blade deterioration — all things that can develop within 3 years of normal driving.

How can I prepare my 2023-plate car for its first MOT?

Check all lights and bulbs, inspect tyre tread depth (minimum 1.6mm), test windscreen washers and wipers, ensure number plates are clean and legible, and look for any windscreen chips in the driver’s line of sight. A pre-MOT check at a local garage typically costs £20–£40. See our full pre-MOT checklist for a complete guide.

Should I book my MOT early to avoid the March rush?

Definitely. You can book your MOT up to one month before the due date without losing any days on your certificate. Booking in late February for a March-registered car means you avoid the busiest period and have more choice of test slots.
