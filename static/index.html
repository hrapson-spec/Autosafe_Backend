Why Check Your MOT Risk Before Your Test?

Every year, around 28% of vehicles fail their MOT in the UK — that's roughly 7.5 million cars. A failed MOT means unexpected repair bills, wasted time, and potentially being left without a legal vehicle to drive. The average cost of MOT failure repairs is between £150 and £500, depending on the issues found.

By checking your risk score before your test, you can identify the specific components most likely to cause a failure on your car. This gives you the opportunity to have issues fixed proactively at a garage of your choice — often cheaper than emergency repairs after a failed MOT. Many common MOT failures, such as worn brake pads, faulty bulbs, or low tyre tread, are inexpensive to fix when caught early.

AutoSafe's prediction goes beyond generic advice. Because we analyse your car's individual MOT history — including past advisories, previous failure reasons, mileage patterns and vehicle age — the risk score is tailored specifically to your vehicle, not just a national average.

Common MOT Failure Areas

MOT testers inspect your vehicle across multiple categories. The most common reasons for MOT failure in the UK include problems with lighting and signalling (blown bulbs, misaligned headlamps), suspension (worn shock absorbers, damaged springs), brakes (worn pads, corroded discs, binding calipers), and tyres (insufficient tread depth, sidewall damage, incorrect pressures).

Other frequent failure points include windscreen chips in the driver's line of vision, excessive exhaust emissions, worn steering components, and structural corrosion. The risk weighting varies significantly by vehicle make, model, and age — for example, older vehicles are far more likely to fail on corrosion and suspension, while newer cars more commonly fail on lighting issues.
